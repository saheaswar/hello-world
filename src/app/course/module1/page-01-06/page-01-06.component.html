Examples of commenalism

Animal and plant Birds commonly build nests in trees for raising their young, which benefits the birds but does not affect the trees. This Baya weaver is a small bird that builds intricate nests that hang from the tips of tree branches. An additional advantage for the birds is that the branches cannot support the weight of predators, so they cannot reach the birds.

Two animals Barnacles are crustaceans that as adults live inside shells attached to objects, such as piers, ships, and many kinds of animals. The barnacles, which are filter feeders, benefit from being anchored to a spot where there is plenty of saltwater containing food. The animals to which barnacles attach are not affected.

Two plants The trunk of this tree provides a home for several kinds of epiphytes, including bromeliads, ferns, and mosses. Epiphytes have aerial roots that cling to surfaces. The epiphytes benefit by being anchored to a place where water and nutrients collect. The trees to which they attach are neither harmed nor helped.
